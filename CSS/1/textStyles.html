Effects

Though companies were hired for work on the pilot episode—such as Entity FX for the visual effects[32]—effects departments later worked exclusively for the series.[33] Ivan Hayden was the visual effects supervisor, and worked with many levels of the production staff. During pre-production, Hayden went through the scripts, looking for possible visual effects. He then had a concept meeting with the writers, and after settling on the effect designs to use, coordinated with the special effects and stunt departments. Supernatural features a synthesized orchestral score, although traditional acoustic instruments such as guitars and cellos are used at times. Special instruments have also been used in specific episodes, such as "bluesy gospel music" played on a broken-down piano in the faith-healing episode "Faith". Unlike other television shows, the series features two composers: Christopher Lennertz and Jay Gruska.[35] Each composer scores every other episode, giving them extra time to write the scores, which usually end up being around 30 minutes per episode. Filming locations

Though the pilot was filmed in Los Angeles, principal filming took place in Vancouver, British Columbia. Thus, on-location filming usually took place in the Lower Mainland. "Dead in the Water" was filmed at Buntzen and the final scenes of "Simon Said" were filmed at Cleveland Dam. Other locations used on the show were often reused two or three times, with the art department making variations to conceal this.
